## Premise
A nuclear bomb detonates in Los Angeles harbor, followed by additional foreign attacks. The U.S. collapses into chaos. Ex-Green Beret Jeff Eriksson leads his family to "the Homestead," an eccentric prepper's fortified compound in the Rocky Mountains (Utah). The series follows the survivors inside the compound as they clash with each other and the violent world outside. Central tension: the ultra-prepared wealthy family who owns the Homestead vs. the military veteran family hired for security.

Setting: Post-nuclear-attack Utah, survivalist fortress compound. Grounded and realistic (no zombies/supernatural). Power grid collapse, fallout, societal breakdown.

## Source Material
Based on the *Black Autumn* book series (10 novels, 300,000+ copies sold) by Jeff Kirkham (28-year Green Beret veteran) and Jason Ross (co-founder of Readyman and Black Rifle Coffee Company). The TV adaptation is a family/faith-friendly version with violence toned down from the military thriller novels.

## Faith Element
Faith is a constant presence. Character Jenna Ross openly wears her Christian faith and narrates journal-style reflections framing events in religious terms. Conversations about God's existence, will, and absence recur. Set in Utah, the story reflects the authors' Mormon preparedness ethos.
